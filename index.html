Walk With Me (2017 - Present)

Walk With Me started as a humble mobile web app built during my last week at the Girls Who Code Summer Immersion Program is now an almost completed, award-winning Android app that uses Google Maps API, Node.js, Firebase Realtime Database, and more to achieve its purpose: protect people who often walk alone, such as city residents and college students, by connecting them to their friends and giving them a virtual guardian. WPI BCB Summer Research Experience (July 2017 - August 2017)

By collaborating with Professor Ruiz and Professor Ryder in the Bioinformatics and Computational Biology Department at WPI, I worked with another student to design and implement a UI and intuitive visualization for SlipBuddy, an Android app that helps people stop overeating by identifying their triggers and alerting user's when their reported stress, sleep, etc. approaches their triggers. FRC 4048 Java Programming (2016 - Present)

As part of the Java programming tea on FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC) Team 4048, I've worked on algorithms to automate the motion of different parts of robots i.e. the large arm this year and the Pixy camera last year. I have used sensors such as potentiometers and gyros as part of feedback loops i.e. PID.

Other Projects

I've used Pygame with Python to make animations, games such as blackjack, and a virtual promposal. During my APCS class in 2016 - 2017, I made sorting programs and a tic tic tac toe solver using OOD principles in Java.

Westborough High School Science Olympiad (2014 - Present)

Asides from being president of my school's team this year and an officer for the last three years, I've worked on technical projects in Science Olympiad. In my junior year, I constructed and programmed a battery-operated, Arduino controlled car for the event Electric Vehicle. I also used a gyro and hall effect sensors as encoders for double feedback PID loops to maintain a straight path and drive a precise distance. I won a bronze medal at the Yale Invitational for Electric Vehicle. During my time as an officer and now as president, I've led the team from 37th in the state to 7th, and this fall, we placed 3rd overall at the Brown invitational.
